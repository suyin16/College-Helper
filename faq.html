FAQ

Basic things to know

What is an Associates's Degree?

An associate’s degree is an undergraduate academic degree awarded by colleges and universities upon completion of a course of study intended to usually last two years. It is considered to be greater level of education than a high school diploma or GED.

What is a Bachelor's Degree?

A bachelor’s degree is a four-year degree meaning it typically takes four years of full-time study to complete your bachelor's degree.

What is a Master's degree?

A master’s degree is a post-secondary or graduate degree that is often pursued following a bachelor’s degree. Even though a master’s degree is much more rigorous and demanding than a bachelor’s degree, it can usually be completed in just two years of full-time study.

What is a Doctorate (Ph.D.)?

A doctoral degree is an advanced graduate degree that typically follows a bachelor's or master's degree. Unlike bachelor's and master's degrees that can be completed on a part-time basis, doctoral degrees are work-intensive and usually require full-time attention.

What is financial aid?

Financial aid helps students pay for college expenses. Financial aid can come in the forms of grants, loans, student employment, tuition payment plans, and scholarships.
