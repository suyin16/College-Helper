Advice for incoming freshmen as a CUNY student

The transition from high school to college may be rough, but here are a few pointers if you're going to a CUNY school:

  • 1. Be familiar with the type of campus you're in and make some friends
  • 2. Make sure you know how you're going to travel on and off campus. Be familiar with MTA routes and keep a map just in case
  • 3. Sign up for classes as soon as possible. Classes will fill up fast
  • 4. Join clubs. It's a good way to make friends
  • 5. Your advisors are great people to talk to whwen you need them. Don't hesitate to ask them for help

Location and Dorming

CUNY colleges have urban campuses, though some campuses may seem more "quiet" or "noisy" than others. Campuses in Manhattan, for example, tend to be more urban-looking than ones in other boroughs. Since CUNY is mainly city-based, dorming options are limited. Not all CUNY schools will haeve dorming options, so if you're in a CUNY school without dorming options, you may have to look into other housing options.
